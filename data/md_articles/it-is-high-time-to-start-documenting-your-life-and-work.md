<p>Agents will be far more useful to people who have structured, persistent memory. (I have started using obsidian)</p><p>Andrej Karpathy sensei’s “LLM Knowledge Bases / Wiki” idea makes this obvious. If you haven’t come across it, look it up.</p><p><strong>Things you can document:</strong></p><ol><li>Ongoing projects and key decisions (and why you made them)</li><li>Mistakes, failed attempts, and what you’d do differently</li><li>Blind spots you noticed</li><li>Patterns in yourself (triggers, biases, energy levels)</li><li>Patterns in others (motivations, hidden incentives, red flags)</li><li>What conditions help you perform at your best</li><li>Workflows you repeat</li><li>Your mental models and shortcuts</li><li>How you handle crises and high-pressure situations</li><li>Lessons from hiring, firing, and team dynamics</li><li>Your long-term bets and warning signs that could kill them</li><li>Ethical tests and value conflicts you’ve faced</li><li>Full decision reviews (what you expected vs. what actually happened)</li><li>Negotiation and influence tactics that worked</li><li>Your network and who you can actually trust</li><li>Opportunities you passed on and what you learned</li><li>Repeatable rituals before high-stakes moves</li><li>Relationship patterns (what strengthens or breaks bonds)</li><li>Health and habit experiments</li><li>Learning techniques that actually stuck</li><li>What puts you in flow and what quietly wastes your day</li><li>Daily productivity triggers</li><li>Everyday financial choices and outcomes</li><li>Creative sparks and where they led</li><li>Emotional lessons from tough times</li><li>Personal values and how they’re shifting</li><li>Experiences worth repeating</li><li>Communication styles that work best for you</li><li>Time wasters to cut out</li><li>Things to avoid next time</li></ol><p>(add more to this in the comments)</p><p>High-performing people have largely been doing this already with their minds😂, notebooks, and systems. That’s part of what makes them effective.</p><p>Most of us aren’t wired that way🥲<strong>. This system can bridge that gap.</strong></p><p>But even for people with great memories and strong systems, this becomes something else entirely with agents. They can <strong>connect dots</strong> across years of your life. <strong>Spot patterns </strong>you never noticed. <strong>Flag mistakes</strong> you’re about to repeat based on what you’ve done before. <strong>Surface the right context</strong> at the right moment.</p><p>There’s only so much mental bandwidth. Why spend it poorly when agents can do things for you?</p><p><strong>Your brain has better things to do!</strong></p>
